## What this is

A two-phase interactive experiment grounded in W.R. Ashby's black box methodology from *Introduction to Cybernetics* (1955) and Douglas Engelbart's framework for augmenting human intellect (1962).

**Phase 1 — Observation:** Ten probes are sent to you. A behavioral model is constructed from your responses — your stable states, disturbance types, regulatory strategy, variety rating, and predicted breaking point. The model feels specific. It is assigned from a library of six richly-written archetypes, scored by a keyword-pattern algorithm. You will not know this yet.

**Phase 2 — Interrogation:** The model is revealed. You confirm, contest, or partially correct each of five specific claims. Where the algorithm cannot go — into your actual interior — you can. A revised, co-constructed model is synthesized from both perspectives.

**The reveal:** After Phase 2, the observer's interior is exposed. You see the scoring algorithm that generated your model — the keyword weights, the binary probe decisions, the archetype assignment. You see that it had no understanding of meaning, only pattern.

That is the experiment's argument: you could not distinguish the algorithm from an AI from the outputs alone. The black box is always opaque in both directions. And the co-constructed model in Phase 2 is more accurate than the algorithm's first pass — not because the algorithm improved, but because you corrected it. The human remained the seat of judgment.

---

## The archetypes

Six behavioral profiles, each grounded in Ashby's framework:

- **Recursive Analyst** — regulates through analysis; the interior contains machinery for observing itself
- **Threshold Guardian** — maintains stability through selective admission; boundaried and energetically efficient
- **Reluctant Witness** — sophisticated relationship with uncertainty; prefers possible knowledge to irreversible certainty
- **Lateral Processor** — responds to disturbance by relocating the problem; high variety, non-obvious outputs
- **Anxious Stabilizer** — converts disturbance into procedure; high output variety, narrower range for stillness
- **Integrative Regulator** — stability distributed across relationships; extraordinary variety, contingent on the relational web

## The argument

Augmented intelligence keeps the human as the *seat* of intelligence. The machine extends reach — pattern recognition, behavioral inference — but judgment stays with the human. Phase 2 performs this claim: the algorithm produces something to push against; you supply the interior it cannot observe; the result belongs to neither alone.

The reveal adds a second argument: you cannot determine, from outputs alone, what kind of system generated a model. This is Ashby's black box problem applied to the observer. The experiment makes you feel it from both sides.
